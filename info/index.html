Info About Crystallis

Crystallis is a planet located 97 light years away from Earth orbiting around the star known as Jargon, previously known as "GJ 9827" before the discovery of life in the system.

Crystallis has a 60 hour day and a 20 Crystallis day (1200 hour) year. These extreme conditions require creatures to be able to have good insulation and smart adaptations to the rapidly changing weather.

Crystallis rests on a 50 degree tilt to vertical, resulting in very extreme seasons. Because the tilt is more than 45 degrees, the arctic region and tropical region intersect, creating the 2 unique bands of the Troptics amd Antroptics.

This creates a large variance in day length and temperature between summer and winter. However, during the equinoxes, the day length will be 30 hours everywhere.

Crystallis has a mass of 1.23 times that of Earth and resides as the third planet from its star, Jargon, and orbit 0.559 AU away from it. It has an average equinox temperature of 18 degrees Celsius. Its atmosphere is similar to Earth's, with a mostly nitrogen rich atmosphere with significant amounts of oxygen and carbon dioxide. The planet's name comes from the crystalline rocks found all over the surface.

Generally, on your visit, if you come during the winter or summer, you will stay away from the troptics, as the weather is pretty extreme and lasts for a while, due to the long daylengths. In these regions, the winter has 60 hours of night, and the summer brings 60 hours of very hot direct sunlight. Typically, we only offer experiences here in the spring and fall. These kind of prolonged extreme conditions give a lot of time for the planet to warm or cool, which causes unpleasant experiences.
