Minimus helencia: "Flying Krill"

(No images can legally be recorded on the surface of Crystallis for non-scientific purposes, so only sketches may be shown.)

Minimus helencia, also known as the "Flying Krill," is a flying krill-like photosynthesizer native to all parts of Crystallis, moving with the wind. They have an solar panel-like sun catcher on its back, of which it uses to get light. It gets nutrients by eating wind pollen, which it often symbiotes with, to carry the seeds until it dies. When they are born, they are tossed and flown around in the wind, where they then grow up. Flying krill never nurse their children, and let them live on their own immediately. They also get their water from simply eating raindrops or snow. They have very little capacity for thinking, with their "brain" just being contained within the neurons they use for feeling, like how some creatures on Earth first evolved brains. They have a very good sense of smell and when they smell predators, they often try to steer away from them, yet this behavior often proves unfruitful. Like all plants and photosynthesisers on Crystallis, it uses the purple retinal for photosynthesis, thought to be used on Earth before chlorophyll.
