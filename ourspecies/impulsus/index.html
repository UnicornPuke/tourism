Impulsus migrantis: "Sky-whale"

(No images can legally be recorded on the surface of Crystallis for non-scientific purposes, so only sketches may be shown.)

Impulsus migrantis, also known as the "Sky-whale," is a migrating airborne creature native to Batque in the winter, and Linarét in the summer. The creature uses its food as fuel to fly around, ejecting hot air out of two funnels in the bottom and back, and dozens of smaller ones on the sides to steer. The animal eats minimus helencia with its large mouth, similar to how whales on earth eat thousands of krill. The animal also has a mirror on its head, that it uses to reflect light, due to the to the fact that it can still experience extreme heat during the migration process. It uses temperature to migrate, like most species. Very few species use day length as phenological markers, since day length can either be very extreme or very limited, with a lot of variety even by just a couple hundred miles north or south. Babies are simply dropped once born, and are expected to save themselves before they reach the ground, with parents often flying a little higher before childbirth to give the baby a better chance at living.
