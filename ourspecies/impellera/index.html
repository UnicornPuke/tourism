Impellera trifecta: "Tri-winged Heli-bird"

(No images can legally be recorded on the surface of Crystallis for non-scientific purposes, so only sketches may be shown.)

Impellera trifecta, also known as the "Tri-winged Heli-bird," is a flying predator that is native to the regions of Ëurand and northern Nolle. It flies around lightly with its helicopter wings, it intentionally falls and eats creatures under it. It is known to also accidentally eat plants. Yet, it is one of the few animals to have an unpleasant reaction to Fertilis quattor. Once it lands on the ground, surrounding its prey, it latches to the ground and closes its mouth parallel to the ground, drags its prey up and chews in the air. But don't worry, they are too small to eat humans and generally ignore things they know are too small to eat. It is known to use its wings to photosynthesize, however this reaction only kicks in if it hasn't eaten in a while, like ketosis on Earth. It has eyes on the bottom of its rock-like body, which it uses to scope out prey. It sees in many wavelengths, seeing infared, visible, and ultraviolet light. However, its depth perception is not the best, since it often confuses the summer form of Duotinctus ëurandia for rocks.
